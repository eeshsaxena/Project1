STEP 7: ENTROPY FILTER
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Shannon entropy formula: H = -Σ p(x) × log(p(x))  (natural log, nats)

Sub-step A: Measure H_param (LLM without any document)
  Ask LLM 3 times: "Can children take aspirin for fever?" (no context)

  Response 1: "Yes, aspirin is traditionally effective for fever."
  Response 2: "No, it may cause serious side effects in children."
  Response 3: "It depends on the age and clinical situation."

  3 different answer categories: {yes, no, depends}
  p(yes) = 1/3, p(no) = 1/3, p(depends) = 1/3

  H_param = -(1/3)×log(1/3) - (1/3)×log(1/3) - (1/3)×log(1/3)
           = -3 × (1/3 × (-1.099))
           = -3 × (-0.366)
           = 1.099 nats ≈ 1.06 nats

Sub-step B: Measure H_aug for E1 (SAFE_FOR)
  Give LLM context: "Aspirin -[SAFE_FOR]-> Children"
  Ask 3 times: "Can children take aspirin for fever?"

  Response 1: "Yes, aspirin is safe for children."
  Response 2: "Yes, it is safe per the document."
  Response 3: "Yes, aspirin is safe and effective."

  All 3 say Yes → only one category
  p(yes) = 3/3 = 1.0

  H_aug_E1 = -(1.0 × log(1.0)) = -(1.0 × 0) = 0.0 nats

  ΔH_E1 = H_param - H_aug_E1 = 1.06 - 0.0 = 1.06
  tau (v4) = 0.25
  1.06 > 0.25 → E1 PASSES entropy filter ✓

Sub-step C: Measure H_aug for E6 (CONTRAINDICATED_FOR)

  Response 2: "No, children should not take aspirin."

  k=1.5, b=0.75, N=6 documents

  df("aspirin") = 6 (appears in all edges)

       = -2.56 + 1 = ... very low (common term)
  But still counts.
