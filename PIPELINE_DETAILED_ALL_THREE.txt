THE ONE-PARAGRAPH SUMMARY
================================================================================

LangChain converts your documents into anonymous text blobs, loses all date
information, retrieves the most keyword-similar chunks (which are often the
oldest, most-mentioned ones), gives the LLM all retrieved chunks including
contradicting ones, and produces wrong answers ~48% of the time on conflicted
corpora. TruthfulRAG v4 improves this by extracting structured facts (triples)
and using graph-based PageRank for retrieval, detecting conflicts and running
entropy filtering — but without year information on edges, conflicting facts
score nearly identically (gap of 0.029) making it impossible to eliminate the
wrong one, so the LLM still receives both, resulting in vague or wrong answers.
TruthfulRAG v5 adds two multiplicative terms to the scoring formula — temporal
decay (e^(-0.08t)) and corroboration (log(1+support)) — which together create
score gaps of 1.0+ between conflicting facts, allowing automatic elimination of
the stale/unsupported document BEFORE the LLM ever sees it, plus a calibrated
3-factor confidence score and full explanation chain, resulting in correct
answers 90-93% of the time with honest uncertainty reporting.
