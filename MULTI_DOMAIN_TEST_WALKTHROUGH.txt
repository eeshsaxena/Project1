── PHASE 1: SCHEMA INFERENCE ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
Auto-discovered schema:

    "entity_types":    ["CELESTIAL_BODY", "SPACE_AGENCY", "SPACECRAFT",

                        "CONFIRMED_ON", "PRIMARY_TELESCOPE_OF"]

── QUERY 1: "How many planets are in our solar system?" ──

  The LLM trained before 2006 might say "9 planets" (parametric memory).
  The corpus says 8 (2006 reclassification).
  H_param for "How many planets?" at temperature 0.7:
    Sample 1: "9 planets"
    Sample 2: "8 planets"  (some LLMs already know the 2006 update)
    Sample 3: "8 planets"

  H_param = 0.636 (moderate confusion — the LLM is partially updated on this)
  After context (Pluto reclassified to dwarf planet in 2006):
  H_context = 0.0 (LLM answers uniformly: 8 planets)
  delta_H = -0.636 → Context DID help! (passes the tau=0.25 threshold)

  Final Answer:
    "There are currently 8 planets in our solar system. The IAU reclassified
     Pluto as a dwarf planet in 2006, reducing the count from 9 to 8."
  Confidence: 79%  ← Highest confidence of all 3 domains!

  WHY: The context actually REDUCED the LLM's uncertainty (delta_H negative),
       which is the ideal case for entropy-based selection. The LLM was partially
       confused and the corpus fact corrected it.

── QUERY 2: "Which is NASA's current primary space telescope?" ──

  Hubble [1990] score:  decay(0.08 × 36) = exp(-2.88) = 0.056  (tiny)
  JWST   [2021] score:  decay(0.08 × 5)  = exp(-0.40) = 0.670  (strong)
  Score gap is massive. JWST wins before contradiction checking runs.

  Final Answer:
    "NASA's current primary space telescope is the James Webb Space Telescope
     (JWST), launched in December 2021. While Hubble (1990) was the primary
     deep-space observatory for decades, JWST now serves in that role with
     significantly greater infrared capabilities."
  Confidence: 77%

WHAT THIS PROVES:
  Schema discovered CELESTIAL_BODY, SPACECRAFT, SPACE_AGENCY vocabulary.
  The Pluto query is the most dramatic: corpus context REDUCED the LLM's
